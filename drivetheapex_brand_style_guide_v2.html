Voice & Tone
Confident
We know the sport. Opinions are stated as opinions, not buried in qualifiers. We don't say "it seems like" when we mean "it is".
Close to the action
Not a spectator brand. The writing feels like it came from someone who was there or is working their way there. Energy, not distance.
Sharp, not cold
Direct. No fluff. But we're fans first, so there's passion underneath the precision. Never robotic, never corporate.
Fan energy + professional form
We write like someone who loves the sport and reads the technical reports. Not a podcaster going off the cuff, not a press release.
Write this
"Ferrari's strategy lost them the race. The undercut window was there and they didn't take it."
Not this
"It's possible that Ferrari's strategic approach may have contributed to a suboptimal result in certain respects."
Imagery Direction
Contrast-first
High contrast, graphic compositions. Controlled light source. Monochrome environment, single saturated subject. Never soft or pastel.
Action or detail
Full-speed motion or extreme close-up technical detail. Avoid the generic wide paddock shot. The camera should feel like it earned its position.
Color treatment
Desaturated backgrounds, single saturated hero element. Red-orange tones favored. Always dark overlay before text is placed over any image.
The hero image reference nails it: black and white track, one orange car burning through. That's the brand. Dark world, one thing that cuts through it. Use that as the mental filter for every image decision.
